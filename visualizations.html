Visualizations

These are the point leaders this season.
Liverpool lead all teams by a good margin and have taken the title for the 2019-2020 season.

This graph represents the average attendence to see each team.
Manchester United has the most fans in attendence followed by Arsenal and West Ham United.
However, that has changed in the later months after COVID-19 spread.

It is clear to see that the top scorers in the EPLare players such as Jaime Vardy,
Danny Ings, Raheem Sterling, Mo Salah, and Aubameyang. However, if you look
at other goals per minutes played, you will find outlier Sergio Aguero.
While he's missed most of the season, he scored a remarkable 16 goals in 26 games.
He is the only player in the Top 20 goal scorers to play less than 1500 minutes.

Many great playmakers are represented in this graph.
But let's just cut to the chase: Kevin De Bruyne is on the top of pack
in terms of assists and it's not even close.
However, we shouldn't diregard the contributions of players
such as David Silva, Mo Salah, and Trent Alexander-Arnold.

Teams that have chemistry and link up well can make plays and score goals.
And a good way to tell how well a team plays is to consider the amount of passes
since this stat is often linked to possesion and good passing percentage.
Teams at the top, like Manchester City and Liverpool, are like well oiled machines.

Possesion in nine-tenths of the law, but it's also important to move the ball up the pitch.
The players that have a high total passing distance show willingness and vision.
Players like Virgil van Dijk and Trent Alexander-Arnold should be recognized.

Defense is the other part of the game and clean sheets don't just have to do with the keeper, but also with defense and midfield.
And as you can see, the teams with the best defense and chemistry, like Manchester City and Liverpool top the table again.
It's also nice to see teams like Burnley and Sheffield United doing well and being recognized for their defense and ability to keep the ball out of their own net.

It's easy to remember a lackluster performance from a goalkeeper than a competant one, especially when your team loses.
And what a better way to demonstrate this point than with David de Gea, who was blasted for his terrible performance against Chelsea in the FA Cup Semi-Final, and rightfully so.
But we must also acknowledge his overall effectiveness as a goalkeeper too as he is 5th in terms of % of Shots on Goal Saved.
Nonetheless, Lloris takes top prize in this category.
